# Task 16: Research roguelike progression systems

## Manager notes
Study progression systems in popular roguelike games (Vampire Survivors, Hades, Risk of Rain 2, etc.) to understand how they pace power growth, manage difficulty scaling, and keep runs feeling challenging throughout.

## Current Game State (Wizard Duel)

Before analyzing other games, here's our current progression baseline:
- **XP curve**: Base 20 XP, ×1.25 growth per level (exponential)
- **Level-up reward**: Pick 1 of 3 upgrades (guaranteed 1 class-specific)
- **Enemy scaling**: Count = 5 + wave×3, HP = base + floor(wave/4), Speed = base × (1 + wave×0.02)
- **Damage**: Flat upgrades (+1 or +2), multiplicative buffs (Berserker 1.5×, crit 2×)
- **No diminishing returns** on any upgrade stacking
- **No time-based difficulty** — only wave-based
- **Boss every 5 waves** with HP = base + wave×4

## Research Findings

### 1. Vampire Survivors — Rapid Escalation with Controlled Ceiling

**Core loop**: Survive 30 minutes. Kill enemies → XP gems → level up → pick upgrades → evolve weapons.

**XP Curve (stepped linear)**:
- Level 1→2: 5 XP needed
- Level 2→20: +10 XP per level
- Level 20→40: +13 XP per level (plus 600 XP step at level 20)
- Level 40+: +16 XP per level (plus 2400 XP step at level 40)
- This is **NOT exponential** — it's stepped linear, making early levels fast and late levels slow but predictable

**Reward frequency**: Players level up approximately every 23 seconds on average. Psychology research shows many small gains produce more happiness than one equivalent big gain.

**Power ceiling control**:
- Hard cap of 6 weapons + 6 passives (12 slots total)
- Weapons max at tier 8, passives at tier 5
- First 6 chests are hardcoded (1-1-3-1-5 items) to set high expectations early, then normalize
- Meta-progression caps at ~2.5× damage and ~2× health multipliers

**Enemy scaling**: Time-based, not kill-based. Enemies get progressively harder every minute. At minute 30, a "Reaper" enemy appears that is functionally invincible, creating a hard end.

**Key insight for Wizard Duel**: Vampire Survivors uses a **flat/linear XP curve** (not exponential) which keeps level-ups frequent. The challenge comes from enemy time-scaling, not from slowing down rewards. The power fantasy IS the game — but it has a hard time ceiling (30 min) and hard slot limits.

---

### 2. Hades — Skill-Based with Voluntary Difficulty

**Core loop**: Fight through 4 biomes → boss each biome → escape. Each room offers a visible reward choice.

**Within-run progression (Boons)**:
- Boons from gods modify abilities (not flat stat boosts)
- Rarity tiers: Common → Rare → Epic → Heroic → Legendary → Duo
- Each boon transforms gameplay rather than just adding numbers
- Pom of Power increases boon level (marginal stat boost to existing boon)
- Player agency: can SEE rewards before choosing rooms

**Difficulty scaling (Pact of Punishment)**:
- After first clear, unlock voluntary difficulty modifiers
- 15 conditions, each with multiple ranks (total Heat up to 60+)
- Examples: Hard Labor (+enemy damage), Lasting Consequences (−75% healing), Extreme Measures (enhanced bosses)
- Players choose WHICH difficulty axes to increase — customizable challenge
- Higher Heat = better rewards (bounties), creating progression incentive

**Meta-progression (Mirror of Night)**:
- Permanent stat upgrades purchased with Darkness (currency)
- Examples: +backstab damage, +death defiances, +dash distance
- Paired choices (pick A or B per slot) — prevents "unlock everything"

**Key insight for Wizard Duel**: Hades succeeds because difficulty is **modular and voluntary**. The player controls the challenge. Boons are **qualitative** (change how abilities work) rather than just quantitative (+damage). This prevents flat power scaling.

---

### 3. Risk of Rain 2 — Time vs. Power Race

**Core loop**: Collect items across stages. Difficulty increases with time AND stages. Rush vs. farm tradeoff.

**Difficulty coefficient formula**:
- Difficulty increases continuously with **real time** elapsed
- Each stage completed multiplies the coefficient by 1.15 (compounding)
- Difficulty settings: Drizzle (×1), Rainstorm (×2), Monsoon (×3)
- Multiplayer adds additional scaling per player

**Item stacking (3 types)**:
- **Linear stacking**: +X per stack (simple, for damage items)
- **Hyperbolic stacking**: Diminishing returns formula for percentage-based effects (prevents 100% proc chance or invulnerability)
  - Formula: `1 - 1/(1 + bonus×stacks)` — approaches but never reaches 100%
- **Exponential stacking**: ×0.85 per stack (for cooldown reduction)

**The core tension**: Time always works against you. Spending time farming items makes you stronger BUT enemies scale faster. Optimal play requires knowing when to leave a stage. This creates the "race against the curve."

**Key insight for Wizard Duel**: The **time-based difficulty** is the critical differentiator. It forces urgency and prevents "turtle and farm" strategies. **Hyperbolic stacking** on percentage-based upgrades prevents infinite scaling. The compounding stage coefficient (1.15×) means difficulty accelerates, not just increases.

### 4. Cross-Game Pattern Analysis

#### Pattern A: Frequency of Rewards
| Game | Avg. Reward Interval | Type |
|------|---------------------|------|
| Vampire Survivors | ~23 seconds | Level-up pick |
| Hades | ~60-90 seconds | Room clear → boon |
| Risk of Rain 2 | ~30-60 seconds | Chest/item |
| **Wizard Duel (current)** | **Variable, early fast** | **Level-up pick** |

**Takeaway**: Successful roguelikes maintain reward intervals under 90 seconds. Our XP curve (×1.25 exponential) slows down quickly — by level 10 the threshold is ~186 XP, which is very slow against enemies dropping 1-3 XP each.

#### Pattern B: Diminishing Returns
| Game | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| Vampire Survivors | Hard slot cap (6+6), weapon tiers cap at 8 |
| Hades | Pom of Power gives diminishing returns per level |
| Risk of Rain 2 | Hyperbolic stacking on %-based items |
| **Wizard Duel (current)** | **None — all stacking is linear/unlimited** |

**Takeaway**: Every successful game limits power growth through diminishing returns. We have NO such mechanism — stacking Spell Power gives +1 every time with no ceiling.

#### Pattern C: Difficulty Scaling Dimensions
| Game | Dimensions |
|------|-----------|
| Vampire Survivors | Time → enemy count, speed, new types; Hard cap at 30 min |
| Hades | Fixed rooms → harder biomes; Voluntary Heat modifiers |
| Risk of Rain 2 | Time + stage compound; enemy level = difficulty coefficient |
| **Wizard Duel (current)** | **Wave-only: count + HP + speed (all linear)** |

**Takeaway**: Our enemy scaling is purely wave-based and linear. No time pressure, no compounding, no qualitative shifts. Players can take infinite time between waves.

#### Pattern D: Power Fantasy Timing
| Game | Early Game | Mid Game | Late Game |
|------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Vampire Survivors | Vulnerable (1 weapon) | Growing power (3-4 weapons) | God mode (evolved weapons) → Death |
| Hades | Baseline kit | Build synergies | Execute build vs. final boss |
| Risk of Rain 2 | Scrounging | Building momentum | Either dominating OR overwhelmed |
| **Wizard Duel** | Baseline spells | Upgrading spells | Overpowered quickly, no challenge |

**Takeaway**: All three games have a deliberate "power fantasy peak" followed by either a hard ending (VS) or escalating challenge (RoR2, Hades). Our game reaches peak power fantasy too early with no counterbalancing force.

## Key Recommendations for Wizard Duel

### 1. Add Diminishing Returns to Stacking Upgrades
**Pattern**: Risk of Rain 2's hyperbolic stacking
**Implementation**: For percentage-based upgrades (cooldown reduction, crit chance, etc.), use `1 - 1/(1 + base×stacks)` instead of linear addition. For flat upgrades (damage), consider logarithmic scaling after the 3rd stack.

### 2. Add Time-Based Difficulty Component
**Pattern**: Risk of Rain 2's time coefficient
**Implementation**: Track elapsed game time. Enemy HP and damage should scale with both wave number AND time. This punishes slow play and creates urgency. Even a simple `timeMul = 1 + elapsedMinutes × 0.05` would add meaningful pressure.

### 3. Flatten the XP Curve (Linear Instead of Exponential)
**Pattern**: Vampire Survivors' stepped linear XP
**Implementation**: Replace `×1.25` exponential growth with stepped linear growth (e.g., +15 per level early, +20 mid, +30 late). This keeps level-ups coming at a consistent pace. Players should level up at least once per wave through mid-game.

### 4. Add Power Caps / Slot Limits
**Pattern**: Vampire Survivors' 6+6 slot system
**Implementation**: Consider limiting how many times a specific upgrade can be stacked (max 3-5 stacks per upgrade). Alternatively, introduce an upgrade "tier" system where taking the same upgrade enough times evolves it into a qualitatively different version (like weapon evolution in VS).

### 5. Scale Enemy Quality, Not Just Quantity
**Pattern**: Hades' biome-specific enemies, VS's enemy type introduction timing
**Implementation**: Introduce new enemy types and behaviors at specific wave thresholds rather than just scaling HP/count. Wave 1-5 should feel fundamentally different from wave 15-20 in terms of enemy composition and attack patterns.

### 6. Create a Defined Run Arc
**Pattern**: Vampire Survivors' 30-minute structure
**Implementation**: Define a target run length (e.g., 20 waves / ~15 minutes). The difficulty curve should be designed so that reaching the end feels like an achievement. Add a climactic final encounter rather than infinite waves.

### 7. Make Upgrades More Qualitative
**Pattern**: Hades' boons that change ability behavior
**Implementation**: We already have class-specific upgrades and ability modifications. Expand this — more upgrades should change HOW abilities work (new projectile patterns, AoE → single target, etc.) rather than just adding +damage. This gives players meaningful choices even in late game.

---

## Summary of Patterns

The three most impactful changes for Wizard Duel's progression, based on this research:

1. **Diminishing returns** (hyperbolic stacking) — prevents runaway power scaling
2. **Time-based difficulty** — creates urgency and prevents turtling
3. **Flatter XP curve** — maintains reward frequency throughout the run

These three changes address the core problem (power fantasy peaks too early) from both sides: slowing power growth AND accelerating challenge growth.
